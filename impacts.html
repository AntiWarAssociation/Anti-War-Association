Effects of War

Social Impacts Bibliography

Short Term Effect



Some short term effects of war include the destruction of buildings

and infrastructure.

Other impacts can include human casualties and a temporary decrease of military strength as

they are being used to fight.

Long Term Effect



Forming and breaking of alliances - a change in the relationships of nations or states,

and in some cases can cause an economic crisis.

Other impacts can include PTSD and other forms of psychological trauma on the people,

as well as healthcare and education challenges

Although war is not a positive thing, it usually gives way to technological advancements.

War may also give an opportunity for lifelong relationships

to be formed in a time of suffering and hardship by people who look out for each other. In the same way that war can unite, it can also divide - people

who are from a country being fought against are seen as an enemy, as seen in Australia in WW1,

which introduced the war precautions act. Depending on who wins a war, people in a situation

like this may be forced to leave their country.
